## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: The site avoids the worst tells. No identical card grids (Problems and Services are genuinely differentiated layouts), no gradient text, no hero metrics template, no side-stripe borders. The copywriting is specific and sharp. Two patterns read as trained defaults, however:

1. The warm beige body + terracotta accent palette is the most directly named anti-reference in this site's own PRODUCT.md: "the whole warm-tinted beige body with terracotta accent combination read as default." The execution is polished, but the palette choice reads as the reflex, not a deliberate departure.

2. "SOUND FAMILIAR?" and "BEFORE YOU REACH OUT" are uppercase tracked eyebrow labels on 2 of the 6 sections. They're inconsistently applied (neither a full system nor fully absent), and the all-caps letter-spacing styling is in the saturated AI lane.

**Deterministic scan**: 2 findings from `detect.mjs`:
- `flat-type-hierarchy` flagged in `app/api/contact/route.ts:217` — **false positive**. API route handlers have no visual typography; the scanner hit code strings. Dismiss.
- `em-dash-overuse` flagged in `app/layout.tsx` — **partially valid**. The em-dashes in `layout.tsx` are in code comments and in the page title (`AZDEVS — Web, App...`), which is a conventional separator and not a copy violation. The real violations are in visible body copy strings in `components/Problems.tsx:198-199` and `lib/constants.ts:102,125` — 4 em-dashes in user-facing text the detector did not locate precisely.

## Overall Impression

The site is clean, fast, and specific enough to stand out from generic agency templates. The copy earns trust through numbers and directness. The Services dark panel is the most distinctive section on the page and shows real design character. The two biggest gaps: zero proof of past work (the primary conversion barrier for a skeptical audience), and a palette that the brief itself calls out as the default to avoid.

## What's Working

1. **Services dark panel** — Stark contrast, clear pricing, service names in uppercase, accordion rows. This section has genuine identity. Nothing else in the dev-studio category looks like this.

2. **Copy specificity and voice** — "No fluff, no bloated agencies," "100% yours," "2 to 4 weeks," "$15,000 for a site that should cost $2,000" — real numbers, real positions, real human voice.

3. **Problems section asymmetric layout** — One featured card spanning two rows avoids the identical-card-grid trap. The "We can fix this →" CTA hint as a progressive reveal works well.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] No social proof — zero evidence of past work**
- **Why it matters**: The primary persona (skeptical small business owner who's been burned by an agency) needs validation before booking any call. The site makes strong quality claims but offers nothing to back them: no portfolio, no case studies, no client names, no testimonials, no before/afters. This is the biggest conversion gap.
- **Fix**: Add a work sample or testimonial section between Services and About. Even one project note ("Redesigned a GTA property management company's booking system — shipped in 3 weeks, retained for ongoing support") carries weight. If NDAs prevent showing work, a single first-name testimonial with business type is still effective.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable craft social proof section`

**[P1] Palette is the explicit anti-reference from the site's own PRODUCT.md**
- **Why it matters**: PRODUCT.md directly names "the whole warm-tinted beige body with terracotta accent combination" as what to avoid. `#F7F6F2` body + `#C85A1E` terracotta is precisely this combination. A design-literate visitor will read it as the 2024-2026 AI landing page default, undermining the "the site IS the proof of work" principle.
- **Fix**: Three viable paths — (a) Move body to near-zero-chroma white (#FAFAFA) while keeping terracotta as the accent, removing the warm tint; (b) Commit to the dark strategy the Services section already uses — treat near-black as the primary surface for hero + dark sections, warm cream as a secondary relief tone; (c) Push terracotta further as a drenched hero surface instead of a cream-accent pattern.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable colorize`

**[P2] Hero section dead space below the left column**
- **Why it matters**: At 1280x900 the left column ends at the CTAs roughly 55% down the viewport. Below the buttons is 150+ pixels of empty cream before the section transition. The first fold is the highest-value real estate on the page.
- **Fix**: (a) Add a trust micro-signal in the dead zone — a client quote, a "15+ projects shipped" stat, or a logo strip; (b) Vertically center the left column content so the gap feels architecturally intentional; (c) Extend the hero to show a subtle preview of the next section (overlapping card, clipped section header).
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable layout`

**[P2] Em-dashes in user-facing body copy**
- **Why it matters**: Em-dashes in body copy are banned (AI cadence tell). Found in visible strings:
  - `Problems.tsx:198`: "...doesn't just look unprofessional — it's actively costing you customers."
  - `Problems.tsx:199`: "...actually works on mobile — we scope it to what makes sense..."
  - `constants.ts:102`: "You get a clear proposal — what we're building, what it costs..."
  - `constants.ts:125`: "...don't have it fully figured out — that's what the first call is for."
- **Fix**: Replace with colons or periods. "...doesn't just look unprofessional: it's actively costing you customers." Short, punchy, same meaning.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable clarify`

**[P2] Eyebrow labels: inconsistent application and AI-saturated styling**
- **Why it matters**: "SOUND FAMILIAR?" and "BEFORE YOU REACH OUT" are the only two sections with uppercase tracked labels. Four other sections use none. The inconsistency reads as accidental, not deliberate. The uppercase + wide tracking is the canonical AI scaffold style.
- **Fix**: Either commit and apply consistently (with a defined visual system that makes the kicker feel intentional), or remove both. "BEFORE YOU REACH OUT" on Contact reads purely as instruction copy; removing it costs nothing. "SOUND FAMILIAR?" on Problems has more voice — if kept, it should have a consistent companion on 2-3 other sections, or be restyled to break from the uppercase-tracking pattern.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable clarify`

## Minor Observations

- The "We can fix this →" CTA hint in the featured Problems card has no onClick handler — it visually reads as a link but is a `<p>` tag. Either make it scroll to `#contact` or restyle it as static copy rather than an arrow-link affordance.
- The HowItWorks connector lines between step badges are at opacity 0.4 on an already-light border color. On most monitors they'll be invisible. Either increase to 0.7+ or remove them.
- "Book a call on Calendly" in the Contact section names the third-party tool. "Schedule a call" with the calendar icon reads more polished.
- `app/layout.tsx` page title uses em-dash separator ("AZDEVS — Web, App...") — conventional for title tags, not a user-facing violation. Keep.
- The `--color-faint: #aaaaaa` token used for footer tagline text on `--color-footer: #111111` — luminance-based contrast: (0.651+0.05)/(0.004+0.05) = 13:1. Fine. But `--color-dim: #999999` used for footer nav links on the same dark bg — similar ratio, fine.
