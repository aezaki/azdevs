# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small business owners and early-stage founders in the Greater Toronto Area. Non-technical, budget-conscious, and already skeptical from being quoted $15,000 for a site that should cost $2,000. They arrive frustrated — by inflated agency pricing, DIY tool limitations, or developers who disappear after launch. Their job: find someone who can build what they actually need, at a price that makes sense, without the runaround.

## Product Purpose

Convert visitors into a booked 30-minute call. The site exists to make Andrew Zaki and AZDEVS feel trustworthy, reachable, and capable — without the overhead or posturing of a big agency. Success is someone reading the hero and thinking "this is the person I've been looking for."

## Brand Personality

Direct, honest, crafted. Confident, scrappy, human. The voice of a knowledgeable friend who happens to be a developer — not a sales pitch, not a portfolio show-off. The brand talks to non-technical clients like adults, skips the jargon, gives real numbers upfront, and earns trust through specificity rather than authority.

## Anti-references

- **Enterprise SaaS aesthetics** (Salesforce, HubSpot, Webflow's own site): corporate blue-heavy palettes, feature matrices, language that says "solutions" instead of "things that work."
- **Freelancer portfolio clichés**: dark hero with glowing code, terminal aesthetic, "I build things" copy, infinite scrolling showcase grids.
- **Bloated agency sites**: award-winning, world-class, synergy. Six pages of methodology for three services. Made to impress CMOs, not small business owners.
- **AI-generated warm cream + orange scaffold**: eyebrow label on every section, numbered section markers (01/02/03) used as default decoration, identical card grids, the whole warm-tinted beige body with terracotta accent combination read as default. The design should feel like a human designer made deliberate choices — every element earns its place.

## Design Principles

1. **The site IS the proof of work.** Every pixel is an implicit portfolio piece. If the site itself feels cheap or generic, the pitch falls apart. Craft is not optional here.
2. **Specificity kills skepticism.** Exact pricing, concrete deliverables, named timelines. Non-technical clients are already guarded from being oversold; specific beats impressive every time.
3. **Earned trust over stated authority.** Don't claim to be excellent — demonstrate it. No "world-class" or "passionate about craft." Show it through the work.
4. **Human first, studio second.** Andrew built this. That should show. One real person with a clear voice beats a faceless agency tone.
5. **Constraint is a feature.** Small team, limited clients, full attention. Position scarcity as intentional quality control, not a limitation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigation, screen reader support, minimum 4.5:1 body contrast, reduced motion support via `useReducedMotion()` on all animations.
